Russia   Exploring the 'former' superpower

Palace of the people

Metro stations in Russia (specifically Moscow) were specifically designed to be 'palaces of the people' and they certainly live up to that. Stations are adorned with mosaics, chandeliers, and marble.

The most common explanations for this involve the early days of the communist state and while people were accepting that their living spaces were cramped and not what we would refer to as 'beautiful' some of their common spaces would be. So the metro stations were carefully designed to wow the people.

But - controversy exists around the materials used for the station. I was told that due the communist leaders' disapproval of religion many historic churches were demolished - leaving loads of materials to be re appropriated for building palaces.
